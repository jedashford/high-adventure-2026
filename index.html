Self-issue the wilderness permit at the trailhead, then hike in ~6 mi to Alice Lake (or Twin Lakes, one mile farther — a July trip report says Alice gets crowded and Twin Lakes is the better night-one camp).

Climb from Alice past Twin Lakes to the Alice–Toxaway Divide, then descend to Toxaway Lake. Shorter mileage day — time for swimming, fishing, and a fireside. Toxaway is the staging camp for the next morning's big climb.

Sunrise switchbacks up Sand Mountain Pass (~9,400 ft) — reviewers call the short off-trail spur to the ridge top the best view of the whole loop. Then mostly downhill past Edith Lake and Farley Lake. Camp low, or push all the way out if the group is strong.

Hike ~6–7 mi · biggest climb, then downhill

Hike out, drive home return confirmed

Finish the last ~4–5 mi down the Farley Lake valley and the Pettit Lake cutoff back to the cars. Lunch in Ketchum or Twin Falls, home by evening — no night driving, which is exactly why Alex added the fourth day.

No. 04

Safety & Communication

What parents should know about how the group stays safe out there.

Reaching the group

There is zero cell service on the entire loop — all four days. Plan of record: leaders carry a satellite communicator (SOS + two-way text) for emergencies, and parents get an ETA text Thursday when the group is back in coverage (around Twin Falls, mid-afternoon). confirm details at pack check

Storms

August pattern is blue mornings, thunderstorms after lunch. The plan keeps the group over the passes before ~1 PM. Lightning drill: off ridges, spread out, crouch on packs, wait 30 min after the last thunder.

Cold

Lake basins sit at 8,000–8,700 ft — nights in the 30s–40s even in August. Every boy carries a warm layer, beanie, and dry sleep clothes that never get worn hiking.

Water crossings & snow

July reports: creek crossings plus lingering snow near the Edith Lake pass (mostly melted by Aug 10). Crossings are done with hip belts unbuckled, water shoes on, facing upstream.

Bears & critters

Black bear country (no grizzlies). Food hangs in bear bags 100 ft from tents; nothing scented in sleeping bags. The real thieves are chipmunks and gray jays.

Altitude & sun

The trailhead is already at 7,000 ft. Hydration starts on the drive up; sunscreen goes on every 2–3 hours — UV at 9,000 ft burns fast.

Wilderness rules

Free permit (8+ groups need the Forest Service call — see the alert up top) · group cap 12 · catholes 200 ft from water, TP packs out · many lake basins are stove-only, so the fireside may be stars + talk, not flames · dogs on leash through Labor Day.

No. 06

The Trail & Conditions

Rated Hard · 4.8★ from 282 AllTrails reviews · elevation runs 6,755 → 9,709 ft. The loop links Petit Creek, Alice Lake–Redfish Lake, Edith Lake, Yellowbelly Lake, and Petit Lake Cutoff trails — counterclockwise from Pettit Lake.

Trail on AllTrails ↗ Trailhead pin — Google Maps ↗ Wilderness rules — USFS ↗

Know before we go

  • Permit: free — but groups of 8+ must contact the Forest Service in advance; trailhead self-issue only covers smaller groups. Group cap is 12 people (see alert at top).
  • Parking: a recent report says Pettit Lake TH charges $10 — bring cash. Lot fills by 9 AM; we arrive afternoon, so expect overflow parking.
  • Water: lakes and creeks the whole way — group carries filters; each hiker needs 3–4 L of capacity.

No. 07

The Drive

380 miles, about 6 hours door-to-trailhead via I-15 N → I-84 W → US-93 N → ID-75 N (fastest of three route options per Google Maps). Leaving at 6:00 AM puts us at the trailhead around 1:00–1:30 PM with two stops.

LegMilesRouteFuel & food notes
Provo → Tremonton~95 I-15 N, merge I-84 W Leave with a full tank. Easy off-ramps in Brigham City / Tremonton if needed.
Tremonton → Burley~110 I-84 W over Sweetzer Summit into Idaho Snowville is the last Utah gas; long empty stretch follows.
Burley → Twin Falls~40 I-84 W → US-93 N Main stop: gas + restrooms + fast food, ~3 hr in. (Perrine Bridge overlook is 2 min off route.)
Twin Falls → Ketchum~85 US-93 N → ID-75 N through Shoshone, Bellevue, Hailey Last reliable gas: Hailey / Ketchum. Top off every vehicle here.
Ketchum → Tin Cup TH~50 ID-75 N over Galena Summit (8,701 ft), left on Pettit Lake Rd No services. Big scenic overlook at Galena Summit — worth 5 minutes.
