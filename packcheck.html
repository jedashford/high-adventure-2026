No. 01

How Pack Check Works

Run it like a flight check, not a suggestion. Bring a bathroom or luggage scale to the church. Every hiker — boys and dads — passes all eight gates before their pack is trip-approved. Dump-and-justify: everything comes out, and each item earns its way back in.

Gate 1Weigh-in

Loaded pack on the scale, with (empty) water bottles and a 2-lb stand-in for food. Target: ≤ 20–25% of body weight.

Gate 2Fit

Hip belt rides on the hip bones and carries ~80% of the load. Shoulder straps wrap without gaps. Sternum strap clipped.

Gate 3Shoes

Sturdy, broken-in, worn tonight. 12 days left is enough to break in — barely. No fresh-out-of-box shoes on Aug 10.

Gate 4Water

3–4 L of capacity (e.g. 2–3 L bladder + 1 L bottle). Show it. Bottles fit in reachable pockets.

Gate 5Sleep system

Bag rated 30°F or warmer-rated, pad, and a tent assignment. Bag lives inside a trash-compactor-bag liner.

Gate 6Storm ready

Rain jacket + warm layer (fleece/puffy) + beanie. Mountain storms don't care that it's August.

Gate 7Light & sound

Headlamp with fresh batteries, whistle reachable without taking the pack off.

Gate 8Walk test

Two laps around the parking lot, loaded. Watch for limping, strap-tugging, leaning. Fix it now, not at mile 4.

⚠ Permit alert — needs action before the trip

Sawtooth Wilderness rules: groups of 8 or more must contact a Forest Service office for a permit — trailhead self-registration only covers smaller groups. Max group size is 12 people; larger parties must split and stay a drainage or a day apart. With ~7 boys + 4–5 dads we're at or over the line. Alex should call the Stanley Ranger Station (208-774-3000) before Aug 10 — note their hours: Mon / Thu / Fri only, 9–noon and 12:30–3 PM.

No. 07

Sawtooth-Specific Cautions

Storms

August pattern is blue mornings, thunderstorms after lunch. Plan to be over Sand Mountain Pass before ~1 PM. Lightning drill: off ridges, spread out, crouch on packs/pads, wait 30 min after last thunder.

Cold

Lake basins sit at 8,000–8,700 ft — nights in the 30s–40s even in August. Hypothermia risk is a wet boy at sunset: sleep clothes stay in the liner and are never worn hiking.

Water crossings & snow

July reports: creek crossings plus lingering snow near the Edith Lake pass (should be mostly gone by Aug 10). Crossings: unbuckle hip belts, face upstream, creek shoes on. Poles help on snow and scree.

Bears & critters

Black bear country (no grizzlies). Clean camp, food hung or in Ursacks 100 ft from tents, nothing scented in sleeping bags. The real thieves are chipmunks and gray jays — zip the pack.

Altitude & sun

Trailhead is already at 7,000 ft. Hydrate on the drive up, easy pace day one, salty snacks. Sunscreen every 2–3 hours — UV at 9,000 ft burns fast, especially off water and snow.

Rules of the wilderness

Free permit at the trailhead (8+ needs Forest Service contact first — see the alert above) · group cap 12 · catholes 6–8" deep, 200 ft from water, TP packs out · campfires only in existing rings where allowed, and many lake basins are stove-only — plan the fireside as stars + talk, not necessarily flames · dogs on leash through Labor Day.
